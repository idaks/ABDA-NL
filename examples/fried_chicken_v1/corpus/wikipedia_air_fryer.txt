Air fryer -- Wikipedia (paraphrased excerpts)

Overview

Air fryers are small countertop convection appliances that
simulate deep frying without submerging food in oil. A fan
circulates heated air at high velocity around the food, producing
a crisp browned exterior through surface dehydration and Maillard
reactions. The original Philips design combined radiant heat from
a heating element positioned just above the food with forced
convection, marketed as "Rapid Air" technology.

Operating temperatures and cooking principle

Air fryers typically operate at temperatures reaching around
200 C (392 F). Traditional deep frying produces Maillard browning
with oil held between 140 C and 165 C. The air fryer achieves
comparable browning by driving hot air rapidly past the food
surface instead of immersing it in oil. The high air velocity
strips moisture from the outer layer of the food, which is the
primary mechanism by which crispiness is created or restored.

Reheating fried food and takeout

Air fryers are widely used to re-crisp previously fried food,
including takeout fried chicken, french fries, and other breaded
items that have softened during transport. A few minutes at
175-200 C is typically sufficient to drive off accumulated steam
moisture from the breading and restore a crisp texture without
drying out the interior of the meat. Food-media guides frequently
recommend the air fryer over the microwave for reheating fried
chicken, because microwaving steams the breading further rather
than driving moisture off.

Comparison with convection ovens

Air fryers and standard convection ovens share the same underlying
principle of moving heated air past the food. Air fryers are more
compact, preheat faster, and concentrate the airflow in a smaller
chamber, which some reviewers argue yields crispier results on
small batches. Others note that a full-size convection oven or
convection toaster oven can produce equivalent results.

Design variations

Common household air fryer formats include basket-style models
(with a perforated basket over a drip tray), countertop
convection ovens with multiple racks, halogen-lamp models with
glass bowls, and larger barrel-style units sized for whole
poultry.
