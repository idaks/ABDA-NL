Community Airshed Letter (Paraphrased Composite)

To: Regional Forest Supervisor
Re: Proposed prescribed burn, [Unit identifier]

Our community writes in response to the public notice of the
proposed prescribed burn on the subject unit, scheduled within
the upcoming burn window.

We recognize that fire is a natural part of this landscape and
that fuel accumulation has produced elevated wildfire risk. We
are not opposed to forest-health treatments in principle.

We ask the Agency to specifically address the following concerns:

1. *Smoke drift.* The prevailing-wind direction for the proposed
   burn window places our town squarely in the smoke plume. Our
   community includes a disproportionate share of residents with
   asthma, COPD, and cardiac conditions. Previous burns on
   comparable units have produced multi-day PM2.5 excursions at
   the school-district monitoring station.

2. *Burn-day forecast transparency.* We request that the Agency
   publish the 24-hour PM2.5 forecast at the populated-area
   receptor before issuing the final go/no-go determination, and
   that the determination explicitly reference the EPA NAAQS
   threshold.

3. *Alternative treatment options.* We request that mechanical
   thinning options for the unit be documented in the decision
   memo, including cost comparison and timing, so the community
   can understand why burning was selected over smoke-free
   alternatives.

We appreciate the Agency's attention to these concerns. We do not
seek to block forest-health work; we ask that air-quality impacts
on our community be treated as a material factor in the decision,
not only as a regulatory threshold.

Sincerely,
[Community organization signatories, paraphrased]
