NBA Collective Bargaining Agreement (2023) -- Second Apron Summary
Paraphrased public explanation

Cap Structure

The CBA ratified in 2023 introduced a "second apron" above the
luxury tax threshold. Teams with team salary above the second
apron face substantially more restrictive rules than traditional
tax-paying teams.

Key Second-Apron Restrictions

Teams over the second apron are subject to several categorical
prohibitions, including:

- *No taxpayer mid-level exception.* Teams over the second apron
  cannot sign free agents using the taxpayer MLE.
- *No salary aggregation in trades.* Matching-salary rules that
  allow combining multiple outgoing salaries to acquire a larger
  incoming salary do not apply; each outgoing contract must match
  within permitted bands individually.
- *No sign-and-trade acquisitions.* Second-apron teams cannot
  acquire players via sign-and-trade.
- *Frozen future first-round picks.* First-round picks seven
  years out are frozen while the team is over the second apron,
  and can be further demoted if the team remains over the apron
  in subsequent seasons.

Implication for Strategy

These restrictions are categorical, not balancing tests. A team
that has already crossed the second apron cannot evaluate a
stacking-veterans strategy as a trade-off against future
flexibility; the relevant roster tools are simply unavailable.

For formal modeling, this is naturally represented as a strict
rule: over-second-apron status forecloses certain construction
moves regardless of the ordinary competitive case for them.

Strategic Consequence for Rebuilds

The second-apron rules have significantly raised the cost of the
"spend big" rebuild path for teams that would need to stack
veterans via the tools above. For rebuilding teams below the
apron the restrictions do not bind directly, but the rules shape
the ceiling of what contending teams can do and therefore the
market for veteran talent.
