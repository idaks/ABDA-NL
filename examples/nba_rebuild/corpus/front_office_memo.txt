TO: Basketball Operations
FROM: Assistant GM, Strategy
RE: Season Approach -- Recommendation

Summary

The following is a recommendation for our strategic posture for
the upcoming season, written as a framing document for the
basketball-operations meeting next week.

Team Context

- We are not a realistic contender this year. Playoff projection
  models place us in the 25-35 win range under normal rotation.
- Our under-25 rotation -- three players drafted in the last
  three cycles plus one young international signing -- is
  universally graded as a top-5 league-wide young core.
- The upcoming draft is consensus-strong, with clear top-5 talent
  and credible first-round depth.
- We are below the second apron and have two future first-round
  picks available to trade if needed.

Options Under Consideration

1. *Compete.* Play our rotation to win, give heavy minutes to
   the young core in meaningful games, accept that the pick will
   likely fall in the 8-12 range. Prioritize development.

2. *Tank.* Sit or minutes-cap veterans in the second half, target
   a bottom-3 record, accept short-term competitive cost for
   better lottery odds.

3. *Veteran stack.* Use future firsts plus salary aggregation to
   acquire one or two veteran stars to accelerate the competitive
   window. (Flagged: would move us over the second apron; see
   CBA memo on construction restrictions.)

Key Judgments

The 2019 lottery reform substantially weakens the case for
full-season tanking. Moving from a bottom-6 record to a bottom-3
record no longer delivers a large expected pick-position gain;
the last 10 losses of a tank are close to pick-neutral.

The development cost of a tank year for a top-5 young core is
difficult to price but is credibly large. Public career-arc work
supports competitive exposure as a positive factor for players in
our core's age range.

The veteran-stack path is structurally limited by the CBA second
apron. Our modeling suggests we cannot execute a two-star
acquisition without crossing the apron, which then forecloses the
construction tools we would rely on the following year.

Recommendation

Option 1 (compete). Specifically: play the young core meaningful
minutes, trade veterans on expiring contracts only if value is
clearly above replacement, and preserve future first-round
flexibility. Accept a pick in the 8-12 range as the expected
outcome. Reassess at the trade deadline.
