NBA draft lottery -- Wikipedia (paraphrased excerpts)

The NBA draft lottery is an annual event held by the National
Basketball Association, in which the teams that missed the
previous season's playoffs participate in a weighted random
drawing to determine the order of the first fourteen picks in
that year's NBA draft. The lottery has been used since 1985 and
has been modified several times since, most notably by the 2019
flattening reform and the introduction of the play-in tournament.

Purpose and rationale

The lottery was introduced to reduce the incentive for
non-playoff teams to lose games intentionally to secure a higher
draft pick. Before 1985, the worst team in the league was
guaranteed the top pick, an arrangement widely seen as creating
perverse incentives during the latter portion of the season.

Weighting and the 2019 reform

Lottery odds have been weighted since inception to give worse
teams better odds of higher picks, but the specific weighting
has changed over time. From 1994 to 2018, the worst team had a
25.0% chance of the first overall pick. Effective with the 2019
draft, the NBA flattened the odds so that the three worst teams
each share a 14.0% probability of the top pick, and the odds
curve falls more gradually across the remainder of the lottery.

The flattening was motivated by persistent concerns about "late-
season tanking" -- teams deliberately losing games to drop in
the standings. Under the pre-2019 weighting, a team could
meaningfully improve its expected draft position by securing the
league's worst record; under the flattened weighting, a bottom-3
finish yields the same top-pick odds, so losing beyond that
threshold does not meaningfully improve expected pick position.

Effect on team behavior

Analysts have reached mixed conclusions about the effectiveness
of the 2019 reform. Most public analysis treats the reform as
having weakened but not eliminated the tanking incentive: it
remains rational to miss the playoffs in order to secure any
lottery position, but the marginal value of the last ten or
fifteen losses of a tank is close to zero under the new odds
structure.

Play-in tournament

The play-in tournament, introduced for the 2020-21 season,
provides seeds 7 through 10 in each conference with a chance to
qualify for the main playoffs through a short mini-bracket. The
play-in partially addresses tanking incentives from the opposite
direction: teams on the fringe of the playoff picture are given
a competitive reason to continue pursuing wins late in the
season.

Collective bargaining and future changes

Draft lottery mechanics are set forth in the NBA Collective
Bargaining Agreement. The 2023 CBA retained the 2019 odds and
added the second apron and associated roster-construction
restrictions, which further constrain the "spend big to compete
around a veteran core" rebuild path. Future CBA negotiations may
further adjust lottery odds or introduce additional
anti-tanking measures.
