American Gastroenterological Association — Clinical Practice Update
Management of Barrett's Esophagus (2022, paraphrased excerpts)

Background

Barrett's esophagus (BE) is a metaplastic condition in which the
normal squamous epithelium of the distal esophagus is replaced by
intestinalized columnar epithelium. It is a precursor to esophageal
adenocarcinoma (EAC). The annual progression rate from non-dysplastic
BE to EAC is low (~0.1-0.3%) but non-trivial in aggregate.

Recommendation 7: Long-Term Acid Suppression

Patients with Barrett's esophagus should be maintained on long-term
proton pump inhibitor (PPI) therapy at the lowest effective dose.
Acid suppression reduces intra-esophageal acid exposure and is
associated with reduced rates of progression to high-grade dysplasia
and EAC. Observational data (including the AspECT trial, 2018) and
mechanistic studies support this recommendation.

Evidence Grade: A (strong recommendation, moderate-to-high quality
evidence).

Comment

The panel notes that in patients with competing indications --
particularly concurrent antiplatelet therapy following percutaneous
coronary intervention, or elevated fracture risk -- the benefit of
ongoing PPI therapy must be balanced against these specific risks on
an individualized basis. Discontinuation is not routinely advised
solely on the basis of long-term use; BE surveillance and acid
suppression remain standard of care.
