COGENT Trial Summary
Bhatt DL et al., "Clopidogrel with or without Omeprazole in Coronary
Artery Disease," New England Journal of Medicine, 2010.
(Paraphrased summary.)

Design

Double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled trial of 3,761 patients
receiving dual antiplatelet therapy (aspirin plus clopidogrel)
randomized to receive omeprazole 20 mg daily or placebo. Primary
endpoint was a composite of cardiovascular death, non-fatal
myocardial infarction, coronary revascularization, or ischemic
stroke.

Results

- Cardiovascular event rate: 4.9% (omeprazole) vs 5.7% (placebo);
  hazard ratio 0.99 (95% CI 0.68-1.44), not statistically
  significant.
- Gastrointestinal bleeding: 1.1% (omeprazole) vs 2.9% (placebo);
  hazard ratio 0.34, significant reduction.
- Trial was terminated early for financial reasons before reaching
  planned enrollment, limiting power for rare cardiovascular events.

Interpretation and Subsequent Work

COGENT is frequently cited as evidence that PPI-clopidogrel
interaction is not clinically meaningful. Subsequent pharmacokinetic
studies have distinguished between PPIs: pantoprazole shows minimal
CYP2C19 inhibition compared with omeprazole and esomeprazole, and
is generally preferred when concomitant PPI therapy is clinically
necessary.

Formulary Implication

When the underlying GI indication is compelling (e.g., Barrett's
esophagus), substituting pantoprazole addresses the ACC/AHA concern
without discontinuing acid suppression. This substitution is
commonly accepted by cardiology-GI coordinating committees.
