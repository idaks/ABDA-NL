Endocrine Society — Position Statement on Long-Term PPI Use and
Bone Health (2019, paraphrased excerpts)

Summary

Long-term proton pump inhibitor (PPI) use has been associated in
observational studies with modest increases in hip, wrist, and
spine fracture risk. The mechanism is incompletely understood but
likely involves reduced calcium absorption in an acidic-dependent
step and possible osteoclast modulation.

Key Findings

- Meta-analyses of observational studies report hazard ratios for
  hip fracture in the range of 1.2-1.4 among chronic PPI users.
- The effect size is small relative to established osteoporosis
  risk factors (age, prior fracture, corticosteroid exposure,
  low BMD).
- Randomized evidence is limited; residual confounding is a
  plausible alternative explanation.

Recommendation

In patients with elevated fracture risk (postmenopausal women with
low BMD, prior fragility fracture, or systemic corticosteroid use),
clinicians should periodically reassess the indication for long-term
PPI therapy. Continuation remains appropriate where a clear
gastrointestinal indication exists (e.g., Barrett's esophagus,
Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, bleeding peptic ulcer history).

Evidence Grade: C (conditional recommendation, low-to-moderate
quality observational evidence).

(Source: paraphrased from Endocrine Society position statements on
medications affecting bone metabolism. Illustrative only, not for
clinical use.)
