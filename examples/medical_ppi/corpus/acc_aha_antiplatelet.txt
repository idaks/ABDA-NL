American College of Cardiology / American Heart Association
Focused Update on Antiplatelet Therapy After PCI (2021, paraphrased)

Section 4.3: PPI Co-Administration With Clopidogrel

Clopidogrel is a prodrug that requires hepatic activation via the
CYP2C19 enzyme. Certain proton pump inhibitors (particularly
omeprazole and esomeprazole) are potent CYP2C19 inhibitors and may
reduce the conversion of clopidogrel to its active metabolite,
decreasing platelet inhibition.

The writing committee recommends:

- In patients receiving clopidogrel after percutaneous coronary
  intervention (PCI), concomitant use of omeprazole or esomeprazole
  should be avoided when feasible.

- If acid suppression is clinically necessary, pantoprazole or an
  H2-receptor antagonist is preferred.

- The evidence base for this recommendation is mixed: the COGENT
  randomized trial (Bhatt et al., 2010) did not show an excess of
  ischemic events with pantoprazole + clopidogrel, but was
  underpowered for rare events. Pharmacodynamic studies consistently
  show reduced clopidogrel active-metabolite levels with omeprazole.

Evidence Grade: B (moderate recommendation, moderate-quality
evidence with conflicting trial and pharmacodynamic data).
