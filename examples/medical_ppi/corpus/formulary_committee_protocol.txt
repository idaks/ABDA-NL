Hospital Pharmacy & Therapeutics Committee
PPI Continuation Review Protocol (Revised)

Purpose

This protocol guides the review of continued proton pump inhibitor
therapy in hospitalized and ambulatory patients. It is intended
to support, not replace, individual clinical judgment.

Review Triggers

A formal review is triggered when any of the following apply to a
patient on chronic PPI therapy:

1. Recent cardiac stent placement with concurrent clopidogrel
2. New or worsened osteoporosis, recent fragility fracture, or
   initiation of bisphosphonate therapy
3. Patient age >= 75 with no documented ongoing GI indication
4. Medication reconciliation on admission showing PPI without a
   documented indication in the chart

Review Process

The reviewing pharmacist performs the following steps:

1. Confirm indication. Is there a current indication meeting AGA
   criteria (Barrett's esophagus, erosive esophagitis grade C or
   D, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, recurrent bleeding peptic ulcer
   disease, high-dose chronic NSAID therapy with prior ulcer)?
   If not, proceed to deprescribing consultation.

2. Check drug-interaction profile. If the patient is on
   clopidogrel post-PCI, review the specific PPI prescribed.
   Omeprazole or esomeprazole on clopidogrel triggers a cardiology
   consultation and a recommendation to switch to pantoprazole
   unless contraindicated.

3. Check bone-health risk. If the patient has documented
   osteoporosis, recent fragility fracture, low BMD on recent
   DEXA, or chronic corticosteroid exposure, include a note in
   the review for the primary team about long-term PPI risk.
   Lowest effective dose is recommended.

4. Document. The review note specifies which guideline(s) drove
   each recommendation so that the primary team and any
   downstream reviewers can follow the reasoning.

Complex Cases

Cases involving all three triggers (indication present, clopidogrel
interaction, bone-health concern) are escalated to the P&T
committee for formal review. The committee records the priority
ordering it applied and the rationale, to support consistency
across cases.
