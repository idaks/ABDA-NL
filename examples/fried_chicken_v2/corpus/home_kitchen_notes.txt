Home kitchen inventory and usage notes

Small-appliance inventory (current)

- Countertop air fryer (5 L basket model), acquired in the 2024
  kitchen refresh. Located on the counter next to the toaster.
  In regular use for reheating takeaway and roasting vegetables.
- Microwave oven, 900 W. Used for reheating liquids and
  non-breaded leftovers.
- Full-size electric oven with convection mode. Slow to preheat
  (~10 minutes), so rarely used for small reheating jobs.
- Toaster, single-purpose.
- No deep fryer on the premises.

Usage pattern for takeaway reheating

When takeaway arrives from a fried-chicken restaurant, the
default protocol has been to transfer the pieces from the
takeaway box into the air fryer basket for 3-5 minutes at 180 C.
This consistently restores an audible crust and drives off the
condensation that accumulates in the box during transit. The
protocol has been tested with orders from three different chains
over the last year; all produced an acceptable result.

Constraints

The air fryer is a shared resource. When it is mid-cycle on
another item (e.g. reheating sides), the reheat protocol is
unavailable until the current cycle completes. For the purposes
of this scenario, assume the air fryer is free and operational
at the time the order arrives.

Rationale for recording this

This note exists because the argument for ordering fried chicken
to-go rests on the assumption that crispiness can be restored at
home. If the air fryer is absent, broken, or occupied, that
premise fails and the to-go option loses its main supporting
argument.
